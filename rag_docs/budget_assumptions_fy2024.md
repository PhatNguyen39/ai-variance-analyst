# FY2024 Budget Assumptions — NovaTech Solutions Inc.

**Document Type:** Internal Planning Document  
**Prepared By:** Finance & FP&A Team  
**Last Updated:** December 2023  
**Applicable Period:** January 1, 2024 – December 31, 2024

---

## Executive Summary

The FY2024 budget was built on a foundation of continued ARR growth, disciplined headcount expansion, and increased investment in demand generation. Total revenue is budgeted at $14.1M, representing a 22% year-over-year increase. Operating expenses are budgeted at $12.8M, targeting an operating margin improvement from -8% to -3%.

---

## Revenue Assumptions

### Product Revenue (SaaS) — Account 4001
- **Budgeted:** $10.2M annual
- **Growth Driver:** Net new ARR from Enterprise segment ($2.2M target), expansion revenue from existing accounts (NRR assumed at 112%), offset by expected churn of 6% gross annual churn.
- **Seasonality:** Q1 soft (slower enterprise procurement), Q3/Q4 acceleration as annual renewal cycles peak.
- **Key Risk:** Enterprise deal slippage in H1 could compress full-year by up to $800K.
- **ACV assumption:** New logo ACV of $45K average; expansion ACV of $18K average.

### Professional Services Revenue — Account 4002
- **Budgeted:** $2.16M annual
- **Driver:** Implementation projects tied to new Enterprise logos closed in Q4 2023 (backlog of $620K entering 2024). Each new Enterprise logo assumes 3-4 month implementation engagement.
- **Seasonality:** Q2 and Q4 strongest as implementation kicks off post-contract signature.
- **Dependency:** Revenue directly tied to Sales velocity; delay in new logos reduces PS revenue with ~60-day lag.

### Support & Maintenance Revenue — Account 4003
- **Budgeted:** $1.44M annual
- **Driver:** Recurring support contracts attached to active SaaS customer base. Renewal rate assumed at 94%.
- **Seasonality:** Relatively flat with slight Q4 pickup as contracts co-term with SaaS renewals.

## Cost of Goods Sold Assumptions

### Cloud Infrastructure — Account 5001
- **Budgeted:** $1.14M annual ($95K/month base)
- **Assumptions:** AWS infrastructure supporting production, staging, and dev environments. Costs scale at approximately 8% of SaaS revenue. Assumed 10% YoY efficiency gains from reserved instance purchases and architecture optimization.
- **Risk:** Unexpected customer data volume growth or new enterprise deployments mid-cycle can spike costs 20-30% above plan without proportional revenue benefit in the same period.

### Third-Party Software Licenses — Account 5002
- **Budgeted:** $504K annual
- **Includes:** Snowflake, Datadog, Segment, Stripe processing fees. Stripe fees scale with transaction volume; others are largely fixed contracts.

### Professional Services COGS — Account 5003
- **Budgeted:** $1.056M annual
- **Assumptions:** Fully-loaded cost of PS delivery team (3 FTEs + subcontractors). Margin target: 51% gross margin on PS revenue. Subcontractor usage assumed for overflow capacity.

## Operating Expense Assumptions

### Salaries & Benefits — Account 6001
- **Budgeted:** $3.72M annual
- **Headcount Plan:** 42 FTEs entering 2024, net +6 planned hires (2 in Engineering Q1, 2 in Sales Q2, 1 Customer Success Q2, 1 Product Q3).
- **Merit cycle:** 3.5% average merit increase effective April 1, 2024.
- **Benefits load:** 22% employer burden on base salaries.
- **NOTE:** Salary is the single largest cost driver. Any variance here is likely due to hire timing, backfills, or unplanned attrition.

### Sales Commissions — Account 6002
- **Budgeted:** $816K annual
- **Plan:** 8% commission rate on new ACV; 3% on expansion. OTE for 4 quota-carrying reps assumes 100% attainment.
- **NOTE:** Commission variance is a leading indicator of sales performance. Overrun = quota exceeded; underrun = pipeline weakness.

### Marketing & Advertising — Account 6003
- **Budgeted:** $1.14M annual
- **H1 Plan:** Digital demand generation, content marketing, SEO ($420K)
- **H2 Plan:** Major brand awareness campaign, sponsorship of SaaS industry conference in Q3 ($380K earmarked), and digital continuation ($340K).
- **NOTE:** Q3 marketing budget is front-loaded for the annual user conference and brand campaign. Variances in Q3 are expected to be the highest of the year by design.

### Travel & Entertainment — Account 6004
- **Budgeted:** $264K annual
- **Key Events:** SKO (Sales Kickoff) planned for April in Las Vegas ($85K), customer advisory board meetings Q2/Q4, executive travel.
- **NOTE:** April T&E variance is expected and budgeted due to SKO. Unplanned T&E spikes outside of April should be investigated.

### Software & Tools — Account 6005
- **Budgeted:** $216K annual
- **Includes:** Engineering tools (GitHub, JetBrains), Productivity (Notion, Slack, Zoom), Sales tools (Salesforce, Outreach), Marketing tools (HubSpot, SEMrush).
- **Relatively Fixed:** Low variance expected; spikes may indicate unplanned tool additions.

### Depreciation & Amortization — Account 6006
- **Budgeted:** $300K annual
- **Fixed:** Based on existing asset schedule. New hardware or capitalized software development could increase D&A mid-year.

### Rent & Facilities — Account 6007
- **Budgeted:** $420K annual
- **Fixed:** Two office leases (HQ San Jose + NYC satellite). HQ lease renewed January 2024 at $28K/month (3% increase from prior year). NYC office at $7K/month.

### Legal & Professional Fees — Account 6008
- **Budgeted:** $336K annual ($28K/month)
- **Includes:** Ongoing corporate legal retainer, annual audit, IP filings, contract reviews.
- **Risk:** M&A activity, board-level governance matters, or significant customer contract disputes can cause material spikes (50-100%+). These are by nature unpredictable.

## Key Budget Risks

1. **Enterprise Sales Velocity:** Every quarter of pipeline delay compresses SaaS and PS revenue.
2. **Churn:** Each percentage point of additional gross churn = ~$102K annual revenue impact.
3. **Infrastructure Scaling:** Rapid customer growth without reserved instance planning = cost spike risk.
4. **Headcount Timing:** Late hires save salary spend but can create capacity constraints that delay revenue.
5. **FX:** Minimal exposure (~5% of revenue from EMEA); not hedged for FY2024.
