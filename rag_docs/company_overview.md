# Company Overview — NovaTech Solutions Inc.

**Document Type:** Internal Reference  
**Audience:** Finance, FP&A, and LLM context grounding

---

## Business Description

NovaTech Solutions Inc. is a B2B SaaS company headquartered in San Jose, California. The company provides workflow automation and intelligent document processing software primarily to mid-market and enterprise customers in the financial services, healthcare, and professional services verticals.

**Founded:** 2018  
**Employees:** ~42 FTEs (entering FY2024)  
**Stage:** Series B ($28M raised; last round closed Q3 2022)  
**Revenue Model:** Subscription SaaS (85% of revenue), Professional Services (12%), Support (3%)

---

## Products

- **NovaPlatform Core:** The flagship SaaS product. Per-seat subscription with tiered pricing ($3K–$12K ACV per seat tier). Enterprise customers are multi-seat with annual contracts.
- **NovaFlow PS:** Implementation and configuration services sold as a package with new Enterprise logos.
- **NovaSupport Premium:** Enhanced SLA support tier, typically bundled with Enterprise contracts.

---

## Customer Profile

- **Target:** Mid-market to enterprise (200–5,000 employees)
- **Average ACV:** $45K (new logo), $63K (mature account)
- **Average Contract Length:** 12-24 months
- **Top Verticals:** Financial services (38%), Healthcare (27%), Professional Services (22%), Other (13%)
- **Geographic Mix:** Domestic US (95%), EMEA (5%)

---

## Revenue Drivers & Business Dynamics

### What Makes Revenue Go Up
1. New Enterprise logos closed by Sales team (2–4 month sales cycle)
2. Expansion within existing accounts (seat additions, upsells to higher tier)
3. Strong renewal rates (NRR target: 112%)
4. Q3 and Q4 are seasonally strongest for contract renewals

### What Makes Revenue Go Down
1. Customer churn (budget cuts, competitor displacement, M&A)
2. Deal slippage from quarterly pipeline — large deals often push a quarter
3. Downsell (customers reducing seat count at renewal)
4. Delayed implementation start pushing PS revenue recognition

### Cost Structure Dynamics
- **70% of expenses are people costs** (salaries, benefits, commissions)
- Infrastructure costs scale with customer data volume and API usage
- Marketing spend is lumpy — major campaigns in H2 (Q3 conference, Q4 digital push)
- T&E spikes predictably in Q1 (SKO) and Q2/Q4 (customer events)

## Key Business Events FY2024

### Q1 2024 (Jan–Mar)
- Sales Kickoff (SKO) event scheduled for April — preparation costs begin in Q1
- Two new Engineering hires onboarded (backfill for 2023 attrition)
- Annual audit fieldwork begins Q1 (legal/audit fees elevated)

### Q2 2024 (Apr–Jun)
- SKO held in Las Vegas (April) — significant T&E spike expected
- Two new Sales hires ramping
- Customer Advisory Board (CAB) meeting Q2

### Q3 2024 (Jul–Sep)
- **Major brand campaign launched** targeting financial services vertical
- Annual user conference sponsorship (NovaSummit 2024) — Marketing's largest single spend of the year
- Q3 historically shows the highest marketing variance due to event timing
- **Known Issue:** A large financial services customer (representing ~$420K ARR) went through M&A in Q2; churn risk materialized in Q3, creating SaaS revenue headwind.

### Q4 2024 (Oct–Dec)
- Strongest revenue quarter (annual renewal concentration)
- Professional Services backlog converts to revenue as Q3 implementations complete
- **Infrastructure incident:** A batch processing architecture change in September triggered unexpected compute scaling in October and November, causing cloud infrastructure overrun.
- Contract renegotiation with a strategic partner required external legal support (November legal spike)

## FP&A Reporting Cadence

- **Monthly Close:** Books close by business day 5 of following month
- **Flash Report:** P&L summary issued to leadership by business day 3
- **Variance Commentary:** Required for any line item with >10% or >$25K variance
- **Forecast Update:** Quarterly rolling 12-month forecast updated within 2 weeks of close
- **Board Reporting:** Quarterly board deck with budget vs actual bridge analysis
